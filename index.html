When we look at the existing interactions, systems and commands we use to interact with software on popular operating systems (and their desktop environments), we see patterns created early on by their creators.

Think of various versions of Windows, Linux and OSX. Think of how we have command line interactions, where we need to memorize frequently used commands in our mind in order to become skillful at using software.

These decisions were created by people with certain mental models that they acquired through a combination of influencing factors such as: education, environment, opportunity and genetics.

People do not acquire information necessarily in the same combination of influencing factors. Some have stronger skills in spatial recognition, numerical processes, lexical systems and even social interactions.
